THE GOOD BUNNY DOLL SHOP

Limited Collection of Vintage Dolls, Teddy Bears, & Accessories


Established in 1997

Meet Nancy Rogers, also known as Bunny Rogers.

Born in McAlester, Oklahoma, I was a "military brat." Moved to Sindai, Japan, then Tokyo, then Yokahoma, Japan, then Fort Bragg, NC, then Trieste, Italy, then Heidelberg, Germany, then to Goldsboro, NC, then Charlotte, NC, then finally Chapel Hill, NC. Then I moved to Washington DC, and then back to Oklahoma, where I met and married my husband in Oklahoma City at Oklahoma City University. We moved to Fayetteville, GA, where we had 2 beautiful children, one adopted from Korea. Then 20 years later, we adopted two baby girls from Vietnam.

From there, only 40 years after leaving Chapel Hill, I moved back to Chapel Hill, and we are here to stay. Go Heels!

Sometimes I hold back a doll or two, hoping they might not sell because there's still a lot of "little girl" in me. I sometimes say to people, "Buy a doll for your little girl or the little girl in you," so I guess I have a lifelong interest in dolls.

In October 1997, I decided to turn my hobby into a business. I opened The Good Bunny Doll Shop in Fayetteville, Georgia, the only specialty doll shop south of Marietta. Notable designers include those by Madame Alexander, Annette Himstedt, Raikes Bears, Steiff, Julie Good-Kruger, Dakin, Wendy Lofton and Corolle.

My selection of dolls, assorted fuzzy things, and actual "work of art" creations, is so varied, there is something for everyone. Doll collectors may be particular about their selections, almost every one I sell I keep thinking, "Oh, that was the one I was going to keep." Each doll is just so special to me.

The Good Bunny Doll Shop was featured in The Fayette Citizen News, an article written 26 years ago (1998) when I had a brick and mortar doll shop in Fayetteville, Georgia. We closed up the shop in 2003 when I went to Vietnam to adopt 2 little baby girls. One is now working at the YMCA while the other is finishing up her senior year in college.

The reporter made a small error when she said I carried the American Girl Doll. They were only available mail order then, but I did have a lot of wonderful handmade clothes and furniture for them. I loved having the shop then, but taking care of babies was even more fun, even though I was 55 and my sweet husband was 63 when we adopted them!
